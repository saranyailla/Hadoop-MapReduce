Tavares Bonnett, 44, of Fall River was charged with subsequent offenses of possession with intent to distribute heroin and crack cocaine and the use of body armor in a felony after a traffic stop Friday evening.BROCKTON — A Fall River man was charged with distributing heroin and crack cocaine after a traffic stop Friday evening.Tavares Bonnett, 44, was charged with a subsequent offense of possession with intent to distribute a Class A drug, a subsequent offense of possession with intent to distribute a Class B drug and the use of body armor in a felony.At about 6:20 p.m., Brockton police observed a vehicle heading eastbound on Track Street, passing multiple cars on the right. After pulling the car over, police say the driver, later identified as Bonnett, reached behind before they got to the window.Officers frisked Bonnett for weapons and found one plastic bag with 3.7 grams of crack cocaine and two bags of heroin totaling 1.9 grams, police say.Police also recovered a flak jacket from inside the vehicle.In 2013, Bonnett was also charged with possession with intent to distribute a Class B drug, the Taunton Gazette reported.  At his arraignment at Taunton District Court, he was ordered held on $10,000 bail by Judge Gregory Phillips, who cited a 12-page criminal record, five open cases including a rape charge and a history of defaults.Bonnett is expected to appear in Brockton District Court on Tuesday.Staff writer Corlyn Voorhees can be reached at cvoorhees@enterprisenews.com
