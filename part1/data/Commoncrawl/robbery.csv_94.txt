A total of 12 officers carried out a bust in KingswoodA man who was arrested in a police raid of a South Gloucestershire flat has been convicted of an attempted theft.A total of 12 Avon and Somerset Constabulary officers carried out a bust on a second-floor flat in Britton Gardens, Kingswood, on April 4.Officers seized around 40 pills of "suspected prescription medication where no prescription was present", which will be destroyed, a force spokesperson said.The spokesperson said that separately to the pills seizure, a man in the flat, Jeremy Barrett, was arrested for having previously failed to appear in court.The 58-year-old was brought before Newport Magistrates' Court on April 6. He pleaded guilty to an attempted theft and going equipped for theft on February 25.Barrett was handed a 12-month conditional discharge and ordered to pay £85 in prosecution costs and a £15 victim surcharge.The raid came as part of Operation Remedy, a £2million crackdown on dwelling burglary, knife crime and the illegal supply of controlled drugs.The operation, which launched on April 1, involves 100 Avon and Somerset Constabulary officers.
