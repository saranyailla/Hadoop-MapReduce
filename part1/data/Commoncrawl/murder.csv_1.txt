                                        Madison attorney Andrew Hitt, a former senior adviser to then-Gov. Scott Walker, has been elected chairman of the Republican Party of Wisconsin, party officials announced Saturday.Hitt succeeds Brad Courtney, who stepped down after eight years in the post.Former state and national Chairman Reince Priebus called Hitt the "right choice" at a critical time for Wisconsin Republicans "as our state could very well decide the next presidential election."Hitt "will bring a fresh perspective to the Party while ensuring we stay true to our mission," Priebus said in a news release.Andrew Hitt (Photo: Republican Party of Wisconsin)Hitt is senior counsel at Michael Best & Friedrich and the chief operating officer at Michael Best Strategies.He was elected treasurer of the state party in 2016 and has served as its first vice chairman and interim chairman.In 2015, Walker appointed Hitt to the University of Wisconsin Hospitals and Clinics Authority Board and was on the review board for Wisconsin Supreme Court candidates to replace Justice David Prosser in 2016.Hitt was an assistant district attorney in the Milwaukee County District Attorney's Office before he became Walker's deputy legal counsel and senior adviser.RELATED: Walker appoints political committee treasurer to UW hospital boardRELATED: Scott Walker names finalists for Supreme Court vacancyRELATED: Brad Courtney stepping down as Wisconsin Republican Party chairmanContact Talis Shelbourne at (414) 223-5261 or tshelbourn@jrn.com.
