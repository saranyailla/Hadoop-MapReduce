A man has been charged in connection with the incidentA police officer was taken to hospital after an alleged Derby city centre attack.Two PCs were allegedly attacked in Victoria Street during the early hours of Sunday.They were responding to reports of a fight at around 3.30am when the alleged incident happened.A spokesman for Derbyshire police said: "Officers had responded to a report of a fight in Victoria Street at 3.30am and on attendance detained a man who is reported to have assaulted them."It is understood that one of the officers was taken to hospital as a precaution but was later released.John Newton from Chambers Street, Derby, was charged with two counts of assaulting a police officer, resisting arrest and being drunk and disorderly in connection with the incident.Newton is due to appear at Southern Derbyshire Magistrates' Court, in St Mary's Gate, Derby, on April 30.Did you witness the incident?
